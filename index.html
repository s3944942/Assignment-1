Project Idea

Overview:

My idea for a project is one that I’ve already had lingering in my mind for some time, it’s something that fascinates me due to its subject matter and also its use of IT. My idea is to have a highly searchable and customisable database of the individual performer, production, and songwriting credits for songs. I plan on calling this the DISC (Database of Individual Song Credits), no I did not pick this just for the name… maybe a little bit. This will allow the user to take really deep dives into an artists performing discography to find out the nitty gritty about who played what where and for who.

Motivation:

I am a music nerd, I’ll admit it, and I’ve always loved listening to an album and reading the liner notes on who plays what instruments on each of the songs, allows me to further appreciate the musicianship on some of my favourite albums. I also happen to know there are many other people that like doing the same in music circles, especially in the circles for music genres like jazz, where individual performers and soloists gain legendary status. However, with the rise of streaming, the sales of physical media have gone down significantly, according to the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA), sales for physical media are the lowest ever since they started counting in the 1970s, and thus making it harder to find out who is playing on recordings without doing some digging, while Spotify and YouTube are rolling out features to help identify performers, it’s still hard to do specific searches on what else these musicians have played on.

Description:

The way my project would work is by having a website that users can interact with the database on. The homepage would give the user options on what they which to search by having a drop-down box allowing them to search for Artist, Performer, Songwriter, Album, Song. After the user has searched for an Artist for example, if they haven’t clicked on an option from the auto-complete, then they will be presented with a page with a list of artists with similar names. Once an artist has been selected, the user will be greeted with a photo of the artist, a brief summary of the band (to help differentiate bands with the same or similar names), and a list of albums by default, or if the user desires they can switch to another panel of the artist’s songs. If the user chooses an album then they will be presented with a track list of the album and given the option to have a quick overview of the song credits under each song, with the option to click on any given song title to go to a dedicated page for the song. On said page there will be a list of the performers, songwriters and production team for any given song, from there you can click on any of the performers’ names and be taken to a page where the user can either view the full list of albums they’ve performed on in chronological or reverse chronological order, or use the search functions to narrow down that list, by instrument, production role, songwriting and year, as well as viewing stats like who the most common collaborator is, and when the artist was born/died, when they had the most contributions and what their most common contribution type was. The search tools for a performer will provide a drop-down list of artists they’ve played on songs of, where the user can select one, as well as a drop-down list of instruments, and a multi-slider to select the year range. There will also be advanced options, like choosing other performers to combine with, to see what songs both performers have played on.

Tools and Technologies:

For this project, I will need a relational database management system, such as to store all the data and relay the information from queries that the user makes, I will likely use PostgreSQL or SQLite, just depends on which one I prefer using. Not to mention I will need a server to host this data for the user to connect to, which will likely be done over AWS. I will also need the actual data of the song credits, which I’m considering gathering in a few ways, the most time consuming of which is manually typing out the information into a spreadsheet and then importing into the database. This method would be the easiest, I don’t have to worry about scraping a website like Wikipedia, where the formatting for albums credits is very varied. Another option would be scraping a site like MusicBrainz, which does have all the information sorted in a specific way, yet is missing a lot more than Wikipedia. I will probably do the former.

Skills Required:

To achieve this project, I will need to further learn how to program HTML, as my current skills are very basic and lacking in finesse, not to mention I will have to learn how to use SQL and work within that. I will also need to learn more about website design, as a lot of the music database type websites out there have a really aged appearance, and I would like my website to be different by looking modern and very accessible.

Outcome:

The outcome of my project is allowing the everyday person or intense music nerd to look up and compare information on the performances on their favourite songs and albums. This also benefits the artists by giving them more recognition and exposure towards work they’ve done that would’ve been relegated to the depths of the CD booklet. My project would also allow for people to find new music by searching the songs that have been written by a certain songwriter, and using that list to find other songs written by that person that they might not have heard before.
